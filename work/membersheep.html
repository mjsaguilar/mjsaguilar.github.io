Membersheep:
Subscription Management

Designing a solution to help users easily manage all their subscriptions in one platform — and never get caught off guard by a forgotten free trial again.

Role
Lead UX/UI Designer
Platform
iOS Mobile
Timeline
5 Months
Tools
Figma, FigJam
Overview

The problem

In today's increasingly digital lifestyle, subscriptions have become a daily necessity — streaming, software, fitness, lifestyle. But this convenience has introduced a growing problem: subscription chaos and financial mismanagement.

People sign up for free trials and forget to cancel. Autopay quietly drains accounts. There's no single place to see what you're actually paying for each month. Membersheep was designed to fix all of that.

01
Subscription Chaos
Users have subscriptions scattered across multiple apps, banks, and email confirmations with no central place to track them all.
02
Forgotten Free Trials
Without timely reminders, users frequently get charged after trial periods end — a small but frustrating and avoidable expense.
03
Financial Blind Spots
Autopay makes it easy to lose sight of where money is going. Users often don't realize how much they're spending on subscriptions monthly.
Solution

One place for everything

Membersheep is a mobile app that lets users manage all their subscriptions in one platform — by connecting a bank account or card, or entering details manually. Users can track free trials, get notified before they're charged, and cancel subscriptions without the usual friction.

Feature 01
Home Dashboard

A curated summary that instantly shows users the full picture of their subscription activity — total spend, active subscriptions, and upcoming charges all at a glance.

Feature 02
Add New Subscription

A simple step-by-step process that guides users through selecting and inputting their subscription details seamlessly — no friction, no confusion.

Feature 03
Track Free Trials

The trial tracker lets users monitor remaining trial periods and get notified before they convert to paid — so they can make informed decisions without being caught off guard.

Finding 01

Add Subscription flow

The "Add a Custom Subscription" button placement confused testers. It was removed and replaced with a cleaner "Continue" button — with the custom flow flagged for a future iteration.

Reflection

What I learned

Even as a conceptual project, Membersheep was a rich learning experience. It reinforced that design isn't just about how something looks — it's about creating purposeful, functional, accessible experiences rooted in real user needs.

  • Usability testing is non-negotiable — both before/after iterations came directly from watching real people use the prototype. Without testing, those friction points would've shipped.
  • Accessibility deserves more attention from day one — in hindsight, I'd apply WCAG standards more rigorously from the start rather than as an afterthought.
  • Future scope — advanced spending analytics and personalized subscription recommendations based on usage patterns are natural next features to explore.
