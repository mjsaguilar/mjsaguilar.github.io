Milkyway:
Freemium to Premium

Designing a conversion strategy for a music streaming startup — turning free users into paying subscribers through seamless onboarding, compelling in-app CTAs, and a premium experience worth paying for.

Role
UI/UX + Brand
Platform

Overview

The problem

Milkyway Music is a startup that launched a freemium music streaming product two years ago. They had a strong free user base — but converting those users to paying subscribers was stalling. Two core UX problems were getting in the way.

01
No CTA at signup
The existing registration flow had no call-to-action to subscribe at the moment of highest intent — when a user first signs up.
02
Weak in-app conversion
Free users had no compelling reason or clear path to upgrade once inside the app. Premium felt invisible.

Design

Wireframes & branding

Given the 3-week timeline, I moved quickly from flows to wireframes in Figma, skipping hand sketches to get to testable screens faster. After wireframing and testing, I developed the full brand identity — a process that involved a full pivot from an initial concept called XYLO Cadence to the final Milkyway Music brand.

Testing

2 rounds of usability testing

I conducted 2 rounds of moderated usability testing with 5 participants each via Zoom and in-person. All participants successfully completed every task with no significant errors. Two small but meaningful friction points surfaced:

!
"Skip" button confusion
Users perceived the skip button on the music player as "prohibited" — a negative signal. The label and iconography needed rethinking to communicate its actual purpose clearly.
!
Missing welcome moment
After signing up for the free trial, users expected a "Welcome to Premium" confirmation screen — not to be dropped straight into the music player. A celebratory moment was missing.

Reflection

What I learned

Milkyway was my third project as a UI/UX designer and the one where I most clearly felt my skills growing — particularly in UI craft and the ability to design for a specific business outcome rather than just a user need in isolation.

  • Plan your time — the time pressure surfaced gaps in my planning. Next time I'd build a clearer sprint schedule from day one to avoid stress and missed details.
  • UI kits save hours — spending too long building components from scratch is a trap. Leveraging existing kits early frees time for higher-order design decisions.
  • Commit to your style guide early — changing the entire brand direction mid-project (XYLO → Milkyway) was expensive. A stronger initial commitment to a direction saves significant rework.
