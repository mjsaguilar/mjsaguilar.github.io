My story

IT degree,
design heart

Growing up, I was always drawn to design — interior spaces, architecture, arts and crafts. Anything that involved making something beautiful or solving a problem creatively, I was there. But somewhere along the way I convinced myself that passion wouldn't pay the bills, so I studied Computer Information Technology at CSUN instead.

It didn't take long to realize IT just wasn't for me. I didn't love it the way I loved design. But here's the thing — that detour wasn't wasted. That technical foundation became my secret weapon. Most designers can't read code. I can build it. I understand how products actually get made, which means I design with implementation in mind from day one.

"I didn't just discover design — I came back to it. And I brought everything I'd learned along the way."

After completing my UX design bootcamp at Springboard, I've been freelancing, interning, and building — and I haven't looked back. Every project is a chance to bring something I imagined to life, and that never gets old.
